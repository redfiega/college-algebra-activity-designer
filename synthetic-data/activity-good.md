# Activity: Asymptote Detectives (Jigsaw)

> SYNTHETIC DATA — This is a sample activity used for testing the evaluation system.
> Expected evaluation result: ALL dimensions score 4 or 5.

## Learning Objectives
- Factor rational functions and identify holes vs. vertical asymptotes
- Distinguish between the three cases for horizontal asymptotes
- Communicate mathematical reasoning to peers

## Time Estimate
- Introduction: 5 minutes
- Group work: 18 minutes
- Debrief: 7 minutes
- **Total: 30 minutes**

## Materials
- One large tabletop whiteboard per group (groups of 3)
- One dry-erase marker per student (different colors if possible)
- One printed "function card" per student (see Setup)

## Setup Instructions
Print and cut the function cards before class. Each group of 3 students gets one set
of three cards — Card A, Card B, and Card C. Each card has a different rational
function. Do NOT let students see each other's cards during the individual work phase.

Card A: f(x) = (x² - 4) / (x² - x - 2)
Card B: g(x) = (x² - 9) / (x² + 5x + 6)
Card C: h(x) = (2x² + x - 1) / (x - 1)

## Student Instructions

**Phase 1 — Individual Work (8 minutes)**
You will receive one function card. Keep it face-down until told to begin.

When time starts:
1. Factor both the numerator and denominator of your function completely.
2. Simplify by canceling any common factors. List any restrictions.
3. Find: any holes, any vertical asymptotes, the horizontal asymptote (if any),
   the x-intercept(s), and the y-intercept.
4. Record all your findings in your section of the group whiteboard. Use your color
   marker so your work is clearly yours.

**Phase 2 — Group Synthesis (10 minutes)**
Without showing your card to your group:
1. Each person explains their function and their findings to the group.
2. As a group, decide: What do all three functions have in common? What is different?
3. Write a group conjecture on the whiteboard: "We think the horizontal asymptote
   depends on _____ because _____."

## Collaboration Mechanism
**Jigsaw with split information.** Each student has a different function that no one
else can see. The group conjecture in Phase 2 requires comparing all three functions,
which is impossible without all three students contributing. The individual marker
colors make each person's contribution visible and accountable.

## Stretch Question
All three functions have something surprising in common. Can your group figure out
what it is and explain *why* it happens? (Hint: think about what happens to each
function as x gets very large or very small.)

## Facilitator Notes
- During Phase 1, circulate and check factoring. The most common error is forgetting
  to factor Card C correctly (2x² + x - 1 = (2x - 1)(x + 1)).
- During Phase 2, push groups who write "it depends on the degree" to be more
  specific — what about the degree, exactly?
- Card C has no horizontal asymptote (degree of numerator > degree of denominator).
  Some groups may claim y = 2 (confusing with the equal-degree case). Push back.

## Debrief Prompts
1. What was the difference between a hole and a vertical asymptote in your functions?
   How did you tell them apart?
2. Did anyone's function have no horizontal asymptote? How did you decide?
3. What would happen if we added the functions together? (Stretch — no answer needed.)
4. What was the hardest part of this activity, and why?
