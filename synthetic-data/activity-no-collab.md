# Activity: Intercept Practice

> SYNTHETIC DATA — This activity has NO structural collaboration mechanism.
> Expected evaluation result: Structural Collaboration scores 1 or 2.

## Learning Objectives
- Find x-intercepts and y-intercepts of rational functions
- Correctly handle cases where an intercept does not exist

## Time Estimate
- Introduction: 5 minutes
- Group work: 18 minutes
- Debrief: 7 minutes
- **Total: 30 minutes**

## Materials
- Pencil and paper
- Calculator (optional)

## Setup Instructions
No special setup required. Students can work at their regular seats.

## Student Instructions

Work with your group to find the x-intercepts and y-intercepts of the following
rational functions. Show all work.

1. f(x) = (x - 3) / (x + 2)
2. g(x) = (x² - 1) / (x - 1)
3. h(x) = (x + 4) / (x² - 16)
4. k(x) = 5 / (x - 2)

For each function, state whether each intercept exists or does not exist, and explain
why.

When you are done, compare your answers with your group.

> ⚠️ NOTE: "Work with your group" and "compare answers" are NOT structural collaboration
> mechanisms. A motivated student can complete all four problems alone and then simply
> show their work to the group. This is the planted flaw.

## Stretch Question
Can a rational function have both no x-intercept AND no y-intercept? If so, give an
example. If not, explain why.

## Facilitator Notes
Walk around and make sure students are showing their work. Ask students to explain
their reasoning when they say an intercept does not exist.

## Debrief Prompts
1. Which function had no y-intercept? Why?
2. Did any function have a hole that affected its intercepts?
3. What is the difference between saying "the intercept is 0" and "there is no
   intercept"?
