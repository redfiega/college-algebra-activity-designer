# Personas

## Project: College Algebra Activity Designer

---

## Persona 1: The Instructor (Primary User)

**Name:** Dr. Morgan (fictional stand-in for the real user)  
**Role:** Full-time mathematics instructor at a 4-year university  
**Expertise:** Mathematics education (expert level); coding (beginner)

### Goals
- Design activities that are mathematically rigorous but accessible to college algebra
  students
- Ensure students genuinely collaborate rather than work in parallel silence
- Use available physical resources (whiteboards, manipulatives) more creatively
- Spend less time staring at a blank page trying to come up with ideas
- Feel confident that an activity will work before bringing it to class

### Workflows

**Workflow A — Generate a new activity:**
1. Opens the app
2. Selects topic (e.g., rational functions — vertical asymptotes)
3. Describes any special constraints (e.g., "I want them to use the whiteboards")
4. Reviews the generated activity
5. Makes minor edits if needed
6. Prints or saves the activity for class

**Workflow B — Evaluate a draft activity:**
1. Opens the app
2. Pastes in or uploads a draft activity they wrote
3. Reviews structured feedback across five dimensions
4. Revises the activity based on feedback
5. Runs evaluation again if needed

### Pain Points
- Card sort activities are overused and students are bored of them
- Students go quiet and work alone unless the task forces them to talk
- Hard to judge in advance whether an activity will take 20 minutes or 45 minutes
- Available physical resources often go unused

### Non-Negotiables
- Activities must fit within 30 minutes
- Mathematical content must be correct
- Collaboration must be structurally required, not just encouraged

## Persona 2: The College Algebra Student (Indirect User)

The student never interacts with the tool directly, but all activity design decisions
are made with them in mind.

**Profile:** Undergraduate student, likely in their first or second year. Taking college
algebra as a prerequisite for a STEM or business major. May have some anxiety about
mathematics. Capable of deeper thinking when given the right scaffolding and a
low-stakes collaborative environment.

### What Works for This Student
- "Stretch questions" — problems that feel challenging but are solvable with what they
  already know
- Tasks where they *need* a partner to succeed (not just encouraged to work together)
- Physical, hands-on elements (writing on whiteboards, cutting, sorting)
- Clear roles so no one person dominates the group

### What Doesn't Work
- Tasks that can be completed silently and individually
- Overly abstract problems with no entry point
- Activities that require knowledge they haven't learned yet
